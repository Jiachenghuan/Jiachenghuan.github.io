## TL;DR {#tldr}
If learning depends only on short bursts of motivation, it eventually stalls inside a pile of saved information. A more durable approach is to build a steady loop of input, organization, and output.
## Input: only keep what deserves to enter the system {#input}
### Choose fewer sources {#input-sources}
I would rather keep a small set of stable, revisitable sources than keep expanding the number of entry points.
### Set clear admission rules {#input-rules}
Not every interesting thing deserves a note. I only keep material that can be reused later, directly helps an active question, or triggers a more structured line of thought.
## Organize: turn notes into structure, not storage {#organize}
### Organize by question {#organize-by-question}
Sorting by platform or source is easy, but sorting by question, theme, and ongoing work is far more useful.
### Keep only the minimum context {#organize-context}
Each note should answer three things: what it says, why it matters, and where it could be used again.
## Output: test understanding by restating it {#output}
The value of a learning system is not in collecting material but in making it usable again through summaries, cards, reviews, or small essays.
## Review: give the system a rhythm of iteration {#review}
Review whether your inputs are too noisy, your tags still make sense, and your outputs are actually happening. The point is not a perfect design, but a system that keeps being adjusted.
