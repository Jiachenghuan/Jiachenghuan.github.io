## A lightweight template that can survive long-term use {#lightweight-template}
Reading notes do not need to look like mini research papers. They only need enough shape that you will keep returning to the same structure.
## 1. What question is this piece really trying to answer {#core-question}
Write down the real question first. If the question is clear, your summary will not collapse into line-by-line paraphrase.
## 2. Which arguments are worth carrying forward {#key-arguments}
### Main arguments {#main-arguments}
Keep the two or three claims that hold the piece together rather than summarizing every paragraph equally.
### Supporting evidence {#supporting-evidence}
Only keep the examples, data, or reasoning steps that genuinely changed your understanding.
## 3. Where do I agree, resist, or remain uncertain {#my-judgment}
A note should not contain only the author's voice. It should also store your own judgment, friction, and open questions.
## 4. How will I use this next {#next-action}
End with a concrete next action: turn it into a card, reuse it in an essay, or keep investigating one unresolved claim.
